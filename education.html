Primary school

I attended Shuqun Primary School, where I built a basic foundation in English, Math, and Science. It was a time of figuring things out and realizing I was more of a creative thinker than a math genius.


From 2014 to 2019, I spent a lot of time at student care, which became like a second home. It’s where I made great friends, learned responsibility, and (mostly) finished my homework before playtime.


One of the most memorable moments was an overseas trip to Hong Kong in 2018. It was my first time traveling without family, and it gave me a whole new perspective on different cultures and environments.


I also went on school camps in 2018 and 2019, which pushed me out of my comfort zone in the best way. From night walks to team challenges, those camps helped me build confidence, teamwork, and a lot of fun memories.

Co-Curricular activites

Edison Club

I joined the Edison Club (aka Robotics), where we built robots for different challenges. It felt like diving into a world of futuristic gadgets. It taught me problem-solving, teamwork, and the fun of figuring things out through hands-on learning.

Media club

I was also part of the Media Club, where I got to explore the creative side of storytelling through a lens. We learned how to frame shots, adjust lighting, and capture moments that actually looked cool. Being behind the camera also helped me see how visual design, mood, and motion can be used to tell stories — a skill I now carry over into game design and creative projects.

International School

In Primary 4, I moved to Dubai to live with my mom and continued my studies at The International School of Choueifat - Dubai. Studying in an international setting also gave me a broader perspective on different cultures and ways of learning, which helped shape how I approach challenges today.

Fun fact — while I was studying in Dubai, I also got to learn a bit of French and Arabic! It was definitely a challenge at first , but it was also a cool experience picking up new languages and understanding different cultures through them. (I do not remember anything...)
